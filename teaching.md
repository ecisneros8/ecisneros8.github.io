    <div class="course-title">Uncertainty Quantification for Computational Science</div>
    <p>
      An introduction to the mathematical and computational foundations of
      uncertainty quantification (UQ) with applications to engineering
      simulation. Topics include probability theory and random variables,
      Monte Carlo methods, polynomial chaos expansions (PCE), Bayesian
      inference, variance-based sensitivity analysis (Sobol' indices), and
      surrogate/emulator modeling. Students apply these techniques to realistic
      CFD problems using Python-based frameworks (<em>e.g.</em>,
      <code>chaospy</code>, <code>SALib</code>).
      No prior background in statistics is assumed; a basic knowledge of
      differential equations and programming is expected.
    </p>
